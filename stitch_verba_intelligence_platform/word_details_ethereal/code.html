Ethereal

/əˈTHirēəl/
Adjective

1. Extremely delicate and light in a way that seems too perfect for this world.

"Her ethereal beauty left everyone speechless."

2. Heavenly or spiritual.

"The choir's voices had an ethereal quality that moved the congregation."

history_edu

Word Origin

Early 16th century: via Latin from Greek aitherios (from aithēr 'ether') + -al. Originally used to describe the highest regions of the atmosphere.

Synonyms

delicate exquisite celestial

Antonyms

substantial earthly tangible
